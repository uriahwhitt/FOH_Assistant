X32 Soundcheck Guide

Mapping FOH Assistant recommendations to the X32-Mix / X32-Q tablet apps

X32 tablet interface guide for FOH Assistant soundcheck

How FOH Assistant output maps to the X32 tablet

Sample recommendation — what you'll see in the terminal
Example output [21:34:12] Guitar 1 — Mud cut — low_mid +3.8dB · peak at 315Hz (+2.1dB above band mean — sharp resonance)
  Current:  rms: -11.4dBFS | fader: -3.0dB | eq: EQ Band 2 +1.5dB @ 315Hz
  Genre:    Glam Metal
  Suggest:  Guitar 1: EQ cut at 315Hz, Q≈2.0

Each recommendation tells you: which channel, what type of problem (named move), which Hz to target, and the specific change to make. The sections below show exactly where to go on the tablet.

The display window (--display flag)

Launch with: python main.py --show --display

Shows three curves updated in real time:

ColorCurveWhat it shows
White/grayBoard RTAWhat the board is outputting (post-EQ)
Amber/orangeRoom MicWhat the audience is hearing
Cyan dashedGenre TargetWhat we want for this song's genre

Band highlights behind the curves

ColorMeaning
Warm (yellow→orange→red)Mic reading above target — excess
Cool (blue→indigo)Mic reading below target — deficiency
Dimmed/gray overlayLow-confidence band — unreliable data

Numeric readout strip (bottom)

Per-band dB deviation of mic vs target. Positive = excess (needs cutting). Negative = deficiency (needs boosting).

The curves show normalized shape — position-independent. A +5dB reading means "5dB more than the target expects relative to midrange", regardless of how loud or quiet the overall mix is.
If the display window freezes: close it — it will reopen automatically in about 2 seconds.
Two apps — know which one you're running

X32-Mix

Full-featured. Shows all channels simultaneously. Best for soundcheck — you can see everything at once and navigate freely between channels.

X32-Q

Simplified single-channel view. Faster to reach specific controls but shows one channel at a time. Good during show when acting on a specific recommendation.

The navigation paths in this guide apply to both apps — the steps are the same, the layout differs slightly.
To update the X32 IP address: run python main.py --settings → Session → X32 Connection → enter new IP. The session file stores it automatically — no need to edit YAML files.

Making fader level adjustments

When the recommendation says: "Pull fader to -1dB" or "Suggest: Fader +2dB"

In X32-Mix (recommended for soundcheck)

1
The main screen shows all channels as vertical strips. Find the channel by its label (Guitar 1, Kick, Bass DI, etc.) at the bottom of each strip.
2
Tap the channel strip to select it — the strip highlights and the channel name appears in the detail panel.
3
The fader is the vertical slider in the channel strip. Drag it up or down to move level. The numeric dB value displays at the top of the fader.
4
Target the dB value the system recommended. Unity (0dB) is at approximately 75% of the fader travel — the fader has a non-linear taper, so the top 25% covers only +10dB.

In X32-Q

1
Tap the channel selector at the top of the screen (shows current channel name/number). A picker appears.
2
Scroll to the channel the system named and tap it.
3
The large fader on screen controls that channel's main level. Drag to the recommended dB.
Fader scale reminder: Unity (0dB) is NOT at the top. The X32 fader runs from -∞ at bottom to +10dB at top, with 0dB (unity) at roughly 75% of travel. The system's dB values already account for this — just match the number shown.
If the system says "fader drift +4dB on Guitar 1" — that means the guitarist or someone else already pushed it up. Confirm it's intentional before pulling it back.
Main LR fader (for overall LUFS adjustments)

When the system flags overall loudness ("Room reading 3dB above AOR target LUFS"), it may recommend adjusting the main LR bus, not an individual channel.

1
In X32-Mix: the Master fader is the rightmost large fader on the main screen, labeled "LR" or "Main".
2
In X32-Q: tap channel selector → scroll to LR or Main at the end of the list.
3
Adjust by the dB amount recommended. Small moves here affect the whole mix — use 0.5–1dB increments.

Making EQ adjustments

When the recommendation says: "EQ Band 2 cut to -1.5dB @ 315Hz"

The system will always give you: a band number (1–4), a gain direction (cut = reduce, boost = increase), an amount in dB, and a target frequency in Hz. Here's how to find those controls.

Step 1 — select the channel

1
In X32-Mix: tap the channel strip for the named channel (Guitar 1, Kick, etc.). The channel detail panel opens on the right side of the screen.
2
In X32-Q: use the channel selector at the top to navigate to the named channel.

Step 2 — open the EQ page

3
Look for the EQ tab or EQ button in the channel detail area. In X32-Mix it appears as "EQ" in the tab row at the top of the detail panel. In X32-Q it's a button in the bottom navigation row.
4
Tap it — the 4-band parametric EQ display opens. You'll see the frequency curve graphic and four band controls below it.

Step 3 — find the band the system identified

5
Bands are numbered 1 through 4 from left (low) to right (high) in the EQ display. Band 1 = low end, Band 4 = high end. The system tells you exactly which band to touch.
6
Tap the band in the frequency display graph — a dot or handle appears on the curve. Or tap the band number selector below the graph.

Step 4 — verify the frequency, then adjust gain

7
With the band selected, you'll see three controls: Freq (Hz), Gain (dB), Q (bandwidth). Confirm the frequency is near what the system reported — if it's not close, drag the band handle left/right in the graph to move it to the recommended frequency.
8
Adjust the Gain knob or slider to the recommended dB value. Negative = cut (pull down), positive = boost (push up).
Don't touch the Q (bandwidth) unless the system specifically recommends a Q change. Start with gain-only adjustments — that's what Phase 1 recommendations target.
After adjusting, the system re-evaluates automatically on the next cycle (every 500ms in show mode). No manual recheck command needed.
EQ band type — when to check it

The system reports the current EQ band type it read from the board. Band types on the X32:

Label in appTypeWhen to use
LCutLow cut / high passRemove low rumble below a threshold
LShvLow shelfBroad boost/cut of low frequencies
PEQParametricTargeted boost/cut at a specific frequency — most common
HShvHigh shelfBroad boost/cut of high frequencies
HCutHigh cut / low passRemove high-frequency hash above a threshold
For mid-frequency adjustments (315Hz, 1kHz, 2kHz range), the band should be set to PEQ (parametric). If it's set to a shelf or cut type and the system is targeting a mid frequency, change the type to PEQ first, then set the gain.
When the system says "add a new EQ point at [frequency]"

This means all 4 bands are already in use but none are near the problem frequency. Find the least critical existing band and reassign it:

1
In the EQ display, look for a band with a gain near 0dB — it's doing the least work and is the safest to reassign.
2
Select that band, drag its frequency handle to the problem frequency the system identified.
3
Set the gain to the recommended amount.
4
Set the type to PEQ if it isn't already.

LUFS — Display and Silence Gate Only

LUFS does not drive mix recommendations

The LUFS reading shown in the display window (top right: "LUFS: -21.4") is for monitoring and reference only. The system does not generate "too loud" or "too quiet" recommendations based on LUFS.

Why: mic placement affects LUFS readings significantly. A mic 5 feet closer to the PA reads 3dB higher regardless of mix quality. LUFS is position-dependent and cannot reliably indicate a mix problem.

What LUFS does in this system

•
Silence gate — when LUFS drops below -50dBFS, all recommendations are suppressed (band not playing / between songs)
•
Show log — LUFS is logged every cycle for post-show analysis
•
Display — shown as a numeric readout in the top-right of the display window for your reference
If the overall mix feels too loud or too quiet, use your ears and adjust the main LR fader directly. The system will not prompt you.
Main LR fader location
1
X32-Mix: rightmost large fader on the master section, labeled "LR" or "Main"
2
X32-Q: tap channel selector → scroll to "LR" at end of list
Small moves on the main LR fader affect the entire mix. Use 0.5dB increments and give the room 10–15 seconds to settle before moving again.

Suppressed channels and "no drift" notes

The system suppresses recommendations in some cases — you won't see output for them:

!
60-second cooldown: after a recommendation fires on a channel, that channel goes quiet for 60 seconds regardless of what happens. Don't expect instant re-confirmation after you adjust.
!
Rate-of-change suppression: if a fader moved more than 3dB in 5 seconds, the system suppresses that channel for 60 seconds — it assumes the move was intentional.
!
Sparse mics: channels marked sparse (Drum Vocal, etc.) are ignored when below their inactive threshold. No recommendation fires on a silent mic.
!
Room silence: all recommendations are suppressed when the room RMS is below -50dBFS. Nothing fires between songs.

Soundcheck mode flow
1
Run python main.py --soundcheck --display
--display is optional but recommended — opens the spectrum display window
2
Before band plays — capture empty room ambient:
a + Enter → e + Enter → 60 + Enter
Runs a 60-second background capture. Show loop keeps running.
3
Dial in the board channel by channel as normal. The system gives real-time advisory against the genre target. Recommendation cooldown is 20 seconds in soundcheck mode.
4
During the first full-band passage, run a calibration scan:
cal + Enter
Compares each channel's actual spectrum to the model prediction. Updates instrument priors. Run once or twice during soundcheck.
5
Optional — individual channel isolation (if time allows):
iso 7 + Enter  (Guitar 1)   iso 9 + Enter  (Lead Vocal)   iso 13 + Enter  (Bass)
Solo the channel on the board first, then press Enter when ready. 12-second capture. More accurate than cal scan.
6
When the mix is dialed in, lock the baseline:
confirm + Enter
Saves current channel state. System enters show mode automatically, or launch separately with: python main.py --show --display
